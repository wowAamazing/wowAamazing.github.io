Music Reviews

My thoughts/opinions on an assortment of albums.

I Don't Want to See You in Heaven - The Callous Daoboys
Callous Daoboys in their prime musical lives: this album lives inside my head rent-free most of the time. From wild mathy riffs to catchy pop music changing in and out without any warning whatsoever. This shit makes me feel crazy, like a good crazy, like I need to get naked and dance around my bedroom for the album's length, like I need to start writing cryptic letters to people crazy, like it doesn't matter what comes before or after this moment as everything accumulates into one fine piece of work, one particle made of only time and I am the only one that can experience it. It's great. I would say that I have a favorite track, but it cycles depending on the mood. The switch up in "Idiot Temptation Force" can always get me riled up (in a good way), but also Andrew Spann's and Carson Pace's vocals and the overall "finale"-feel on "III. Country Song in Reverse" scratches an itch I didn't even know I had. A song like "Lemon" is a good one to show the people I know so they don't immediatley run away from me because of the music I prefer; the Fallout-Boy-ideologies on "Distracted by The Mona Lisa" also work for people who are more in tune with their rock. "The Demon of Unreality Limping Like a Dog" has a crazy fucking title and barking and who couldn't ask for that in a metal song (thank you Knocked Loose). "Schizophrenia Legacy" has a very longing but also urgent feel that every album opener needs, which is perfectly followed up with a similar feeling in "Full Moon Guidance." "Two-Headed Trout" has a very familiar synth sample and I can't quite put my finger on where I've heard it first if I've even heard it anywhere first at all and that's not just the a simple Daoboyism; other than that, the song is really catchy and if I had to become a fish with a theme song I would choose this song. "Douchebag Safari" also utlizes catchy synths that make me want to wiggle my finger and sway from side to side. "Body Horror for Birds" is an amazing piece of work and is a great way to calm down before the craziness on the second half of the album. Finally, the whole thing is tied together in a neat bow with both "I. Collection of Forgotten Dreams" and "II. Opt Out" that introduce the entire concept of the "Museum of Failure," a walkthrough of the past that allows for both a reflection and an outlet for grief. This album was my AOTY for 2025 and will remain one of my favorite albums from the Callous Daoboys (opinion pending on what they release in the future). A perfect 10/10.
Orange Mathematics - Frontierer
Probably some of the most concentrated form of noises I’ve ever heard in my life. Out of the gate this album comes at you with the for of about several semi-trucks that have nothing left to lose and are all hyped up on a mixture of rocket fuel, cocaine, and a small collection of opioids. It’s like if classic mathcore got mixed up with sci-fi laser sounds and harsh noise wall at birth and was subsequently raised in the incorrect household for about 2 decades until it grew old enough to recognize its true roots and find its original parents who are somewhere in the alps living their best lives without a care in the world for their original child who then goes on to accept that maybe the true meaning of family isn’t who birthed you but instead who was actually there for you when you need it. The lyrics especially scratch a certain itch that not many other artists can. It’s cryptic poetry screamed as if the speaker is on their dying breath. It’s a very big inspiration for my own style of writing. 
“The tone of what they say will make me shift.”
Very forward. Very loud: the lowest volume setting on my headphones is still too loud for proper safety. It’s like an overdose for your ears. Amazing album to listen to while doing mathematics. A very loud 10/10.
